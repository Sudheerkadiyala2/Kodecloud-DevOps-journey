## Hey, I'm doing this differently.

Most people who start a "100 Days of X" challenge burn out by Day 12 trying to stick to one task per day. I decided to be honest with myself from the start — **I learn better when I'm in flow.** Some days I solve 5-6 challenges in one sitting. Other days I step back and let things sink in. No guilt, no artificial pacing.

This repo is a documentation of my journey through the **[KodeKloud DevOps 100-Day Challenge](https://kodekloud.com)** — every challenge I solve, every mistake I make, and every "ohhh, that's why it works that way" moment I have along the road.

If you're a hirer reading this — this isn't a polished portfolio piece where everything went perfectly. It's a window into how I actually think, debug, and grow. I believe that's more valuable anyway.

## What's In Each Challenge Folder?

Every folder (e.g., `01-/`, `02-/`) has its own `README.md` that covers:

- **What the challenge was actually about** — not just what I did, but *why* it matters in the real world
- **The commands and configs I used** — real, working code
- **What I genuinely learned** — including the things that surprised me
- **Gotchas and mistakes** — the stuff I had to debug and figure out, which is honestly where the real learning happens

Nothing is sanitized. If I hit a wall, I write about the wall.

## A Note on My Pace

I want to be transparent about something: **I don't do one challenge per day.**

On some days I knock out 5-6 challenges when I'm in the zone. On other days, I spend time going deeper on a concept instead of rushing to the next challenge. I re-read documentation. I experiment with things the challenge didn't ask me to try. I break things on purpose just to practice fixing them.

This isn't laziness — it's intentional. I'd rather fully understand 9 challenges than superficially skim through 30. The goal was never to hit a daily streak counter. The goal is to become someone who genuinely knows this stuff.
